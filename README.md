# Bench Power Supply: Code name Durian
Code and Documentation for Variable DC Power Supply Project

## Intro
This is the project that inspired Cornucopia. In April 2025, I found out that using an ATX Power supply, I could make a custom lab bench power supply. I have long had an interest in taking things apart purely for the purpose of seeing how they work, and getting to put them back together. In 2020, that interest focused on computers. I began scouring for anything I could get my hands on: usely old, dusty computers that friends of my parents had lying around their garage. Over time I ended up a sizeable collection of crappy, mid-to-low end workstations that were sitting on people's desks when I was still learning how to draw different letters. But to me they were beautiful. I regularly took them apart, switched around components, and installed various semi-obscure operating systems just to enjoy the process. I even once used one of them as a weight for an at home workout during a zoom P.E. class. Fast forward to the time of writing this, and I regret to say I haven't been able to see any of my components in a few years. After I went away to college, my dad put everything into a storage unit (alongside his furniture and belongings, I don't have *that* much junk). I haven't been home for more than 2 weeks at a time in almost 2 years now, and those components are at the back of my brain when I am there. However, after finding out that my school has a ewaste bin, I've started digging it though it on an odd occasion. I quickly picked back up the habit of grabbing every semi-useful abandoned electrical component I could get my hands on. It made me realize that there are enormous amounts of old, still useful technology that people just throw away. It made me want to reuse it. But how? Enter the ATX power supply. Out of every useful thing you can repurpose old computer components for, by far the most prevalant is the ATX bench power supply. I was sold. It's not a complicated project by any means. It's well documented, mostly consists of purchasing the proper components and assembling them. What made it meaningful to me was the realization that I can do more with it than just following a tech blog or YouTube guide. I could make it something that would have real value 

## Overview
Codename Durian, named after one of the most energy-dense fruits. 
## Project Guidance 
For this project I want to establish goals and constraints to guide me, and document every step for accountability and replicability. 

### Project Goals
- Design, construct, and test a DC power supply using an ATX computer power supply
- Use budget friendly solutions
- Document every step
- Develop my skills in
  - Conducting Actionable Research
  - Documenting ideas and processes in a coherent and meaningful way
  - Establishing maintable workflows 

### Project Rules
- AI must not contribute to this project in any way. NO generative AI for any tasks, including but not limited to:
  - Proof reading, brainstorming, research, formating.
- All designs must be original.
